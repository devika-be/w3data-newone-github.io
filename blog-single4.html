UX/UI Terminology: What Every Designer Should Know

This article is dedicated to designers and the people who have to communicate with them. Of course, if you go into any design studio, 99% of what you see will be drawings. But probably 100% of what you hear in the studio will be words.

Imagine yourself in a situation where:
1. You need a glossary on the blog so you can refer to it reading an article about the elements of user interface.
2. It makes sense to shed light on the most commonly used UX terminology at design conferences and meetups.
3. A new business meeting was going swimmingly – that is, until the client started asking questions about your design process. Then you poured out the complete lexicon of specialized user experience buzzwords the client knew nothing about.
4. While having a conversation about designing stuff with a friend or colleague, it’s helpful if you know basic UI/UX terminology.

The UI/UX Glossary For Designers is a valuable bag of tricks that will teach you to speak the same language as designers. This short article provides you with better understanding of key UI/UX terminology.
